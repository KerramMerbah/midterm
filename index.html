Life of a Monarch Butterfly

Monarch butterflies begin their lives as tiny eggs placed underneath milkweed leaves. After hatching, the striped caterpillars spend most of their time eating milkweed and growing. This plant is important because it is the only food monarch caterpillars can use during this stage of life.

Once fully grown, the caterpillar forms a bright green chrysalis. Inside, it changes completely before emerging as an orange-and-black adult butterfly. The new butterfly waits for its wings to dry, then begins searching for nectar-rich flowers.

Monarchs are best known for their long seasonal migration. Some travel thousands of kilometres from Canada and the United States to forests in central Mexico. No single butterfly completes the entire round trip. Instead, several generations continue the journey, guided by sunlight, temperature, and an inherited sense of direction.

Facts About Monarchs

  • Monarch wings are orange with black veins and white spots.
  • Milkweed protects caterpillars by making them unpleasant to predators.
  • An adult monarch usually has a wingspan of about nine to ten centimetres.
  • Migrating monarchs can travel roughly 80 to 160 kilometres in one day.
